We Have Stardew Valley at Home

Named after the iconic game Stardew Valley and the meme 'we have xyz at home' which is what most parents tell their kids instead of getting them the genuine product the child is asking for, instead offering a meager substitute.
This is a small mobile game that features a joystick controller for the player to navigate the 2D pixel art style environment.
The player controls the little bunny character and aims to collect the grass, carrots and easter eggs which are counted on the UI toolbar.
This game is hardly complete or perfect but a hobby project for my own enjoyment as I learn about mobile game controls and user interfaces.
The player can also enter into the houses by colliding with either of the doors.
To leave the house, collide with the right of the screen.
This game was made using a variety of sprites from the Unity Asset Store. Home screen The player The home screen The minimenu

Thing I'm particularly proud of: The duck pond. The ducks swim back and forth so cutely!

Suki Platformer

This is a 2D platformer game inspired by my dog Suki.
The idea is for the player to use the AWSD keys and space bar to navigate the icebergs, jumping from one to the next.
The space bar is used to jump. The player can eat various items along the way which have unexpected results.
Feeding a dog meat? Yum yum, she might just go FAST!!!
But feeding a dog fish bones or chocolate? Bad dog owner behavior! When the player dies, they respawn at the start of the level, but not before flashing twice to show that they died.
Level 1 takes you across an icy plane, or winter in Finland, whatever you want to call it.
Level 2 however, is not complete. In level 2 I tried my hand at making my own drawing of Suki in Clip Studio Pro, and then making a sprite out of it and animating some breathing and stretching in Unity.
This was the first time I worked with particles for snow and with animations in Unity.
This was made with a variety of assets found online and on Unity Asset Store, as well as one special sprite made by yours truly.
Suki herself Jumping across platforms Level 2 Suki sprite
Thing I'm particularly proud of: The snow particles and Suki sprite in level 2!

Lava Land

This started out as a deliverable for the Unity Essentials Pathway, but ended up becoming more simply because it was so much fun to play around in a 3D space.
This has the player act as a 3D ball and follow from one platform to the next across rope bridges.
The catch? The floor is lava! Fall off of a bride or platform and you plummet into the lava below, dying.
Once you've died, you will respawn at the start point to try again.
The win criteria for the game is colliding with the crystals on the final platform, which calls up a menu asking the player to start again.
Unfortunately, back when I made this I didn't know anything about UIs or changing scenes, so the buttons don't actually do anything, how silly. There are also ash particles blowing across the screen, and the lava below is controlled by an animation in Unity which allows it to appear to be moving.
The skybox adds to the fiery feeling of the world.
The assets in this game come from Unity Asset Store. The start screen & player perspective The fiery environment The crystals on the last platform The menu upon winning
Thing I'm particularly proud of: The lava animation, the ash particles, the respawn.

3D Environment

This was an exercise in building a 3D world using the terrain functionality provided by Unity.
The terrain was painted, the trees, flowers, mushrooms and fallen logs were placed using prefabs and mass placement tools.
It also consisted of a first attempt at making water.
It takes awhile to load in Unity Play, but once it does, you can explore the terrain with the ASWD keys and turn the camera using the mouse.
There is no win citeria here, this was simply the first step in learning to make 3D worlds as I will need one for my thesis project.

Jellyshark


Jellyshark was a deliverable for the Unity Pathways course on Unity Learn.
This was a simple pachinko inspired game, where an object falls into the field at random and encounters obstructions as it follows gravity.
In this case our object is a sushi, and it enters into the field from the top of the screen.
The other game objects provide for collision as they contain colliders.
The sushi will follow gravity, once it lands on the grass, or touches a green jelly, it dies and respawns on top of the screen.
When the sushi collides with a deathly game object, there is a 'beep' sound effect to alert the player that their sushi has expired.
There are no controls in this game and no win criteria.
The objects in the field contain animations, allowing the sharks to swim across the screen and the other objects to rock back and forth, allowing for added randomness of where the sushi will end up.
Thing I am proud of: The animations, respawning and sound effects.
